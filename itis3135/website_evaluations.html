Website Evaluations

The CRAP principles of design are Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity. I used those four ideas to compare one website that feels highly polished and one website that feels crowded and difficult to use.

Apple

Apple is my example of a strong website because the layout is consistent, highly readable, and visually disciplined from section to section.

  • Contrast: Apple uses strong light-dark contrast, large headings, and high-quality imagery to make the most important content immediately visible.
  • Repetition: Buttons, typography, spacing, and content modules repeat in a consistent pattern, which makes the homepage feel unified.
  • Alignment: Content blocks, text, images, and calls to action line up cleanly in a structured grid, so the page feels organized and intentional.
  • Proximity: Related headings, descriptions, and action links are grouped tightly together, while separate product sections are spaced apart clearly enough to scan fast.

Arngren

Arngren is my example of a poor website because it overwhelms the user with scattered links, crowded text, and weak visual hierarchy.

  • Contrast: The page has many competing text colors, image blocks, and link treatments, so contrast is noisy instead of helpful.
  • Repetition: There is very little disciplined repetition in spacing, sizing, or content presentation, which makes the page feel chaotic rather than cohesive.
  • Alignment: Items appear placed almost everywhere on the page without a clear grid, so the reading path feels random and hard to follow.
  • Proximity: Products, labels, prices, and images are packed close together without clear grouping, making it difficult to tell what information belongs together.
